The Problem

A Silent Crisis in Indian Healthcare

  • 18 million deaf & hard-of-hearing individuals in India face acute communication barriers in hospitals.
  • Acute shortage of certified ISL interpreters in healthcare settings across India.
  • Current workarounds — written notes or family proxies — lead to misdiagnoses & compromised patient privacy.
  • Patients lose autonomy over their own healthcare decisions.
"No real-time, bidirectional system exists that converts spoken/written regional languages to ISL animations, and interprets live ISL gestures back to medical text or speech."

Key Field Insights

🏆 Two Dominant Tracks

Landmark-based (edge-fast) vs CNN+Attention (high-accuracy). We combine both for the best of each.

🚩 Critical Gap: Continuous ISL

Field heavily skewed toward isolated/static signs. Real-world continuous ISL is largely unsolved.

💡 Intermediate Rep. Pattern

Keypoint sequences → Gloss intermediary. Papers that skip it degrade badly on OOV words.

Technical Architecture

A Two-Stage Recognition Pipeline

Stage 1 · ISL Recognition

Camera Input→
MediaPipe Keypoints (126 floats)→
Keyframe Pruning (Histogram Diff)→
EfficientNet-B0 Spatial Features→
BiLSTM Temporal Modeling→
Gloss Output (intermediate rep.)

Stage 2 · Translation

Gloss Input→
Encoder-Decoder Transformer→
Text / TTS Output

Why this architecture wins:

  • Keyframe pruning solves the "too many frames" problem on edge/mobile hardware.
  • MediaPipe landmarks (~126 floats) keep input tiny — robust to lighting & background clutter.
  • BiLSTM handles temporal context without needing massive datasets.
  • Gloss intermediary is non-negotiable — systems that skip it degrade badly on OOV words.
  • Bidirectional loop: Doctor speaks + Patient signs — both directions covered.

Avatar Path: Speech → NLP Gloss → ISL Dict Lookup → 3D Skeleton Animation

Recurring Failure Points in Existing Systems

  • ⚠️ Background clutter & lighting variance
  • ⚠️ Signer variation & occlusion
  • ⚠️ Missing non-manual cues (face/head/body grammar)
  • ⚠️ No real-world continuous ISL evaluation
  • ⚠️ Zero clinical deployment in Indian context

SignBridge's Novelty Claims:

  • First bidirectional ISL ↔ Hindi/English system designed specifically for Indian healthcare.
  • Continuous ISL — not just isolated alphabets or single words.
  • Two-stage architecture with gloss as structured intermediary — proven to prevent OOV failures.
  • Robustness-first: MediaPipe landmarks over raw pixels — works in real clinical environments.
  • Avatar-based output — accessible even for tech-naive patients.

Challenges & Mitigations

Risks We've Already Planned For

⚠️ Data Scarcity (ISL)

ISL has limited annotated datasets compared to ASL/BSL — a structural research imbalance.

Mitigation: INCLUDE dataset + custom collection + transfer learning from ASL pretrained models.

⚠️ Real-World Variability

Lighting, background clutter, signer variation in live clinical settings.

Mitigation: MediaPipe landmark-based input (agnostic to background), data augmentation, domain randomization.

⚠️ Non-Manual Cues

Facial expressions & head movements carry ISL grammatical meaning that hands alone can't convey.

Mitigation: MediaPipe Holistic captures face, body, and hand landmarks simultaneously.

⚠️ OOV Medical Terms

Out-of-vocabulary medical terminology not present in the standard ISL dictionary.

Mitigation: Gloss intermediary + finger-spelling fallback + custom medical vocabulary expansion.
